Day 1: What even is the stock market?

I thought that before I start learning about how to go into the stock market, I should know what the stock market even is. After much research, I have learned that the stock market exists primarily so companies can earn capital and funding but it also provides investors with the chance to get in on some of the profits that the certain company makes if it becomes successful. This can have a negative effect though if you buy into the wrong company. To sum it up, It is essentially a marketplace where buyers and sellers can exchange shares of publicily traded companies.

Supply and Demand

Supply and Demand plays a huge role in whether a stock is successful or not. If more people want to buy a stock of a company (say its a new popular startup) than sell it, then the price will move up. Conversely, if more people want to sell the stock then buy it, the prices will go down. This will affect how much your stocks value increases or decreases after your investment.

Ownership

When you buy a stock, you are pretty much becoming a shareholder which gives you a stake in its assets and even its profits (this is where you get dividends from). There are two types of stocks. The first type is Common stock. Common stocks as the name suggests are the most common type of stock. One cool benefit from this type of stock though is that you get voting rights to elect the board of directors at the company you buy into. You get one vote per share so your voting power increases the more stocks you buy. The second type however is Preferred Stock. Preferred Stocks dont offer voting rights and they may have a fixed dividend (Common stocks have variable dividends that increase or decrease depending on the value of the stock). Preferred stocks also usually offer a fixed dividend which would be a more stable investment while variable dividends could go wrong if your company starts losing money.
